Introduction

I'm a P.h.D candidate in the Program of Theoretical and Applied Mechanics (TAM) at Northwestern University. My research has been focusing on studying the failure probability of hierarchical and architectured materials with the help of finite element Monte Carlo simulations and experiments. Under the guidance of my thesis advisor, Prof. Zdeněk P. Bažant, I have established the habbit of always trying to solve problems analytically despite of existing numerical methods at hand. Though being challenging sometimes, it is rewarding in that analytical solutions are like windows, allowing me to see through objects from a brand new perspective.

Apart from doing research, I have worked as the teaching assistant for 3 undergraduate level courses (EA2, EA3 and ME327) and as the grader for 2 graduate level courses (CIV430 and CIV424), during which I have hosted TA office hours, substituted lectures and graded the homeworks.

Research Interests

  • Structure reliability
  • Fracture and size effect of quasibrittle materials
  • Numerical simulation of crack propagation in stochastic heterogeneous materials
  • Novel testing methods for the lower tail strength distribution of materials and structures
